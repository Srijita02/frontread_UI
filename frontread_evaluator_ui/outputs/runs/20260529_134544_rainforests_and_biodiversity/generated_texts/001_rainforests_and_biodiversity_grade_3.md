# Rainforests

Topic: rainforests and biodiversity

Grade: 3rd Grade

Text approved: False

[Word count: 400]  
Have you ever wondered what a rainforest is like? Rainforests are special places filled with many different plants and animals. They are often called the lungs of the Earth because they help clean the air we breathe. In a rainforest, it rains a lot, which helps the plants grow tall and strong. Rainforests are found near the equator, where it is warm and wet all year round. 

One day, Mia and her friend Tom were exploring a rainforest. “Look at that huge tree!” Mia said, pointing up. The tree was so tall that it seemed to touch the sky. “It’s called a kapok tree. It can grow over fifty metres high!” Tom replied, amazed. “That’s taller than most buildings!” They both stared at the tree in wonder, feeling very small beside it. 

As they walked deeper into the forest, they heard strange sounds. “What is that noise?” Mia asked, curious. “That’s a howler monkey,” Tom explained. “They make loud calls to talk to each other. They live high up in the trees.” Mia looked up and saw a group of monkeys swinging from branch to branch. They were having fun, jumping and playing in the treetops. 

Suddenly, they saw a flash of colour. “Wow! Look at that bird!” Mia exclaimed. It was a bright blue macaw. “Rainforests are home to many colourful birds,” Tom said. “They have beautiful feathers that help them hide in the trees.” Mia took out her notebook and began to draw the bird. She wanted to remember how stunning it looked. 

While they were sketching, they noticed tiny insects buzzing around. “What are those?” Mia asked. “They are called butterflies,” Tom replied. “Some butterflies have wings that are as big as your hand! They help flowers grow by spreading pollen.” Mia nodded, understanding how everything in the rainforest is connected. 

As they continued their adventure, they learned about the importance of having many different living things. “Biodiversity means having many kinds of plants and animals,” Tom said. “In rainforests, there are more species than anywhere else on Earth.” Mia thought about how amazing it was that so many creatures lived together in harmony. 

Before they left, Mia and Tom promised to tell others about the rainforest. “We need to protect these places,” Mia said. “They are so important for our planet.” Tom agreed, “Yes! If we take care of rainforests, we help ourselves too.” They smiled, knowing they would share their adventure and the wonders of the rainforest with everyone they met.
